Please tell us about your IT work. What exactly do you do?
I work for Lendlease which is a Tier 1 construction company, I am on the design team, and we do all the ITC/Audio visual and networking design for big commercial towers, hospitals, big infrastructure projects, in every industrial sector out there. If it requires fancy technology, I am involved in it. I am in the building team division of Lendlease, we will put in for bids for big major projects like multi plex, then we will put in a gross maximum price offer based on the documentation that the client gives us. There is a lot of reading a lot of determination from understanding what tech aspirations and requirements are needed, some clients know what they want, for example hospitals, digital departments within health industry are quite advanced in requirement sets. We look at requirement sets in alignment with architectural aspirations of the building. Almost every building now has converged networks, we will put a baseline design which we would then tender if they are successful with the bid.
We put in a gross maximum price with the preferred construction company then if they are awarded the contract they will go and develop design briefs further and then they will come up with performance specs which we will write which will then be divided into the various work packages, generally with work packages we will either edit or review other consultancies work, they are the converged network of the base building, sometimes it involves multiple converged networks because for example some hospitals have an engineering converged network, and a clinical network so there are two layers of networks doing two different functions. They are trying to do a fully converged network so eventually converging networks reduces infrastructure footprint required for the buildings which saves money and time for large projects.
So, there is networking in there, some converged networking, Wi-Fi, desktop surveys, audits, estimation of how many access points are required, inbuilding mobile coverage, requires distributed antenna system that allows mobile coverage withing building, interface and integrated service platforms. The term smart building is a broad and loose term, most of the building management systems, are pretty smart, they do a lot, the integrated service platforms will bring in all the other building systems onto one platform, so you have one data aggregated platform that’s taken all the data and stored it for analytics, so the building industry is jumping ahead trying to find value out of all the data from the building from thousands and thousands of data points they produce daily that adds value to the business.
So, would there be an interface that both employees and clients or visitors use that is all recorded on one system?
Integrated service platforms are called a single pane of glass, so basically within the building and facilities management business’s, we will build the building and hand over to operators or facility managers, sometimes give it over to the owner. Big companies that do it, JLL and spotless etc. these integrated service platforms allow them to manage all the technology and access control, security cameras etc, all from one single platform. Then we can say for commercial towers where there is multiple tenants, we can then give them access to the floors they have leased, they can then get aggregated data and understanding of their usage and utilisation. Autonomous Vehicles

What does it do?

An autonomous vehicle is a vehicle that can navigate and deliver a payload, whether that be people or cargo, without the need for a human to interact with the controls. It can scan and detect its surroundings and react without driver intervention. With a truly autonomous vehicle, a human is not needed to perform any driving function or decision making. the first official autonomous vehicle was developed in 1977 by the Tsukaba mechanical engineering laboratory. Which used an old analogue computer, that used 2 cameras to pick up special lane markings to facilitate autonomous driving. The first autonomous road trip was in 1995 by CMU'S navlab 5, driving across America, from Pittsburgh to San Diego. Driving autonomously for all but 50 of the 2850 miles.


There are multiple levels of automation when it comes to vehicles. These range from level 0 with no automation, to level 5 with full automation, in which even a steering wheel is optional.

  • Level 0 means there is no automation or driver assistance. All control of the vehicle is up to the driver.
  • Level 1 is driver assistance, where only a single automation system is present, such as cruise control.
  • Level 2 is partial automation, where the vehicle has limited control over acceleration and steering. A human driver is still required to monitor the situation and can intervene at any time.
  • Level 3 automation is what we have included is some high end vehicles today, such as a tesla. This is where automation can control the vehicle under the right circumstances, such as on a highway. The vehicle is able to monitor its surroundings and can avoid obstacles and collisions with other non automated vehicles and obstructions.
  • Level 4 is where the vehicle is able to run autonomously in most situations, And is aware of its surroundings and can react to most situations. However a human driver is still required to monitor the situation.
  • Level 5 is complete automation. This is where the vehicle can run autonomously in all situations. A driver is not required, and a steering when can even be excluded.

These days driverless cars are undergoing rapid change and development. Currently autonomous vehicles use a variety of sensors to detect road markings and objects. Radar is used to detect nearby vehicles and other moving objects. allowing for the vehicle to detect if an object is moving toward or away from it. It is used mainly for collision avoidance and other features such as adaptive cruise control and blind spot detection. Lidar uses a laser to scan its surrounding and create a 3d view of the area around the vehicle. Its high accuracy and small processing requirement made it great for quickly determining immediate surroundings. The vehicle uses multiple cameras to view its surrounding and detect vehicle, people, animals and any obstruction that may enter the roadway. It’s also used for object recognition. such as traffic lights, emergency vehicles, construction hazards etc. GPS is used to give the car its global position and a map of its surroundings to facilitate autonomous driving. Right now, the main technological innovation is image and data processing. The onboard cameras are able to gather most of the data used for driving under normal conditions. However, the main drawback is the amount of raw data that needs to be processed and interpreted by the computer. This is slow and right now it is not ideal for situation that require immediate action. Machine learning and neural network will most likely be the technology that moves this forward and allow reliable cheap autonomous vehicles. The other innovation we will see is seamless communication between vehicles. Having the ability to have a direct data link to all vehicles mean that the vehicle will not have to guess what every other vehicle is doing. Saving valuable processing power.

What is the likely impact?

Autonomous vehicles are likely to be a world changing invention. just like electricity or the internet before it. It is set to be one of the biggest game changers in vehicle history. The ramification will be felt in just about every industry.


Just as the introduction of autopilot in aviation has made air travel the safest way to travel, the impact from global adoption of level 5 autonomous vehicles cannot be understated. To be able to hop into a autonomous vehicle and have the confidence that it will be able to react to any situation and keep you safe in your travels will forever change how we use vehicles. In the private sector, it will make tasks like food delivery or package delivery much more reliable and efficient. Ride sharing services will benefit hugely from the adoption of autonomous vehicles. A customer will be able to open an app and be able to request a ride. Then to be able to step into a autonomous vehicle and be able to be delivered anywhere without having a driver will be monumental to the industry. Speaking of industry. Having automated trucking and freight delivery will facilitate a major boom in the freight industry. However, all this novation and automation comes at a cost. It will put millions of people out of a job and will kill the trucking and taxi industry as we know it. The ramification of this will be immense. With millions of people now looking for jobs, unemployment will skyrocket and there will be a backlash towards autonomous vehicles. Some experts say that this will increase the divide between the rich and the poor. With wealthy people being able to enjoy the luxury of autonomous vehicles, while the poor that have been kick out of their jobs and will most likely not be able to afford the autonomous vehicles that took their jobs in the first place.

How will this affect you?

I think this will affect me in many ways. To be able to jump into a car and get some work or study done would be great, or to catch up on some much-needed sleep. To catch an automated bus to a train station and arrive precisely on time will greatly increase my usage of the public transport system. I have insomnia and do not drive when I have been unable to sleep. With autonomous vehicles I will be able to travel allot more. If we had level 5 autonomous vehicles, I would feel a lot safer knowing that most people will be using autonomous vehicles, and that the likelihood of getting into an accident will be drastic lowered. With increased safety and reduced death toll attributed to motor vehicles, car insurance will become a lot more affordable. Currently if i am waiting on a delivery I am given a time frame of when the parcel will be dropped off at my door. With autonomous vehicles that time frame will be a lot more accurate. Machine Learning

What does it do?

Machine learning is an offshoot of Artificial Intelligence (AI), advanced computer systems are used to analyse massive amounts of data from which the machines begin to find patterns and eventually start to make predictions on the tasks given to them. These data sets can be anything from images, to text, to numerical values, likes, dislikes, essentially anything that programmers and developers can feed into the “minds” of the computers.


Tasks that once took countless manhours to complete are now performed in massive quantities and a fraction of the time. Some basic applications of machine learning include software in autonomous vehicles, advertising/marketing, financial security, biometric recognition, medical diagnostics, classifications, and the list continues to grow every day.


The difference between the way the human mind learns, and the way machines learn is the way we process information. Humans learn from our past experiences, whether positive or negative these seemingly insignificant parts of our life impact on how we proceed in the future. Machines are a bit different, being a creation of man, they only have the data we provide them. The more data we give them, the better the ability for them to solve the problem. Depending on what the problem the machines are to solve, different learning techniques must be implemented. These techniques include Supervised Learning, Unsupervised Learning, Reinforcement Learning and Deep Learning.


Supervised learning


As the name suggests supervised machine learning requires supervision to solve the problem at hand. This is done by supplying the machine data with both labels and features. For example, the machine is fed a picture (the data) of a cat (the label) and features including things like shape, colour, size etc. Eventually, with the use of algorithms, the machine itself will be able to associate certain features and determine whether the data presented is a cat or not.


Unsupervised learning


Unsupervised learning occurs when large sets of unlabelled data are presented to the machines. The data is then analysed, and the computers begin to form some sort of structure to make sense of. Depending on the data set it will look for patterns and similarities and extract useful features from it. This learning technique is used mostly for more complex tasks compared to supervised learning. It is useful for when data needs to be classified first and labelled later. This may be the case in scenarios where the machine is trying to find anomalies in massive data sets.


Reinforcement learning


This learning technique takes more of a biological approach towards machine learning. Through feedback given to the machines they are able to navigate around problems to reach their desired outcomes. These computer systems are given either positive or negative feedback on their decisions, and through trial and error, they can build their own framework to complete the task at hand. The reinforcement technique shares many similarities with the supervised learning model although it does have some limitations. Without reinforcement the machine is limited to what data it is fed and can never achieve higher intelligence than the person programming it. Reinforcement learning allows the machine to go above and beyond the capabilities of the human mind and skillset.


Deep learning


Deep learning is a technique that uses a neural network inspired framework and layering to find patterns through large sets of unstructured data. These machines are designed to emulate the human brain and how it functions, and in doing so, can solve much more complex problems and find patterns other techniques cannot. This technique is helping us discover new technologies in medical diagnosis, automated vehicles, and biometric recognition, to name a few. With such complex algorithms deep learning machines require massive volumes of data compared to other more rudimentary techniques.


As machine learning continues to make unprecedented advancements on the technological front, we, as a human race, are living in a very exciting time in history. On a daily basis we are exposed to the powers of machine learning without even realising it. Everything from targeted marketing to traffic directions, all influence us to some extent. Project Idea

Windows Program and Setting Profiler

Overview

Our project idea is to create a Windows program and settings profiler. This will help automate and make it easier for people, and by extension companies who need to reinstall Windows or are installing Windows on a new PC.


The program itself will create and store a profile of the settings and programs that the user can save and use when they want to install Windows or change Windows computers. These profiles can be stored locally or a cloud server which can be linked to an account such as a Microsoft account or a Google account.


We plan to make a user interface that is straightforward and easy to use for those who are not so tech savvy, we plan to do this by making step by step instructions that will be able to guide even the most tech illiterate through the process. We also will have an option for advanced settings that will allow more dedicated computer enthusiasts a more tailored transfers of data such as choosing which settings they would like to leave as default. We believe we will be able to implement and achieve success with this program using basic programming in MYSQL, C++ or Java.


While our tool is will be able to be used by everyday users it will also be able to be scaled up to accommodate larger companies whilst they update their systems. This will not only save business countless man hours in the IT department, but it will save any confusion that the average office user may experience while they try to adapt to an unfamiliar interface.


We hope that eventually our product can expand and work its way into sectors outside of the home and office. Out Tool has the potential to increase efficiency in sectors such as education, medicine, finance and more.


Motivation

Constant technological advancements of the hardware and software of our devices are rendering our once reliable computers into a state of obsolescence. The average laptop is said to last anywhere from 3 to 5 years while desktops have a life of around 4.5 years. Over the course of a 50-year career a professional user of windows may use anywhere from 10 – 16 devices. With each purchase, our machines come with a stock standard version of Windows, and we are left to rebuild our own interface which for many can be quite time consuming.


Recent numbers from Microsoft indicate that there are 1.4 billion monthly users of windows 10 and 11 alone. With the average computer lasting around the 4-year mark it is estimated that there are 350 million installations of Windows every year. For each one of those installations the user would have to spend the time re-installing software, drivers, programs, and their personal data back to the way they have been accustomed to. Further time is then spent personalizing said programs into the users desired settings.


The countless man hours saved in large corporate computer system upgrades, translates into a large financial savings. Companies will have the opportunity to redirect these saving into other sectors of their business all while increasing efficiency.


Description

This program will help with the setup of any new Windows device or help with the reinstallation of an existing Windows install. It will leverage already existing featured tools, often used by system administrators to install Windows in a pre-configured fashion.


During day-to-day use, if the user was to introduce a program to their device or where to change a particular setting, our tool would prompt the user if they would like to save setting to their profile. This allows the interface to evolve with the users’ needs. This preferred setting can either be store locally on a device such as an external hard drive or USB or may be able to be accessed through a cloud provider for an additional fee.


Whilst users are able to store their profile data locally, for privacy and security reasons we do recommend the use of using our cloud storage provider. This significantly decreases the risk of a security breach and loss of personal information. A 3rd party platform such as the user’s Google or Microsoft account will allow access to their own data and further increase their online security.


When it comes time for the user to either booting up their new PC or for whatever reason have had to reinstall Windows, they will have to use a windows boot drive to complete the build. Our program will work from within a folder and in conjunction with the windows installation options.


Once Windows has been installed, during the initial setup process, the user will be prompted to choose whether to download their profile from the local drive or to access their profile through the cloud provider. From there the user will be met with an easy to navigate graphical user interface (GUI). Options ranging from a step-by-step setup for the less technically inclined to more advanced setting for user who would like to make changes to their hardware settings, including but not limited driver and BIOS updates.


Once the profile has been agreed to by the user, our program will then download and install all programs and software need to complete the setup from their respective websites. Once downloaded the personalized setting for each individual program will then be change to the preferred user settings. Finally, the user’s stored data is transferred onto their device ready for use.


A “Guardian” feature may be incorporated if needed. This will allow guardians of children or the elderly, to be able to restrict access to certain programs or to lock settings in place so there may be no accidental profile updates.


We will create platform that will allow the user to share their personalized setup. This will give the option for other users to test out and potentially mirror the Interface if they wish to. This will be limited to settings and programs only. All personal information and data will obviously be off limits to others.


Tools + Tech

Considering we are trying to target all users from the everyday gamer to the hardcore PC enthusiast and even into big business and the corporate world, we have options to suite any needs. Dedicated hardware for the user can be as simple as a device and an internet connection, this will cover profile transfers that are 100% cloud based. The option for users to use their own localised storage devices is there, but we can offer no form of security when it comes to their data and personal information.


The use of cloud services in particular IaaS, PaaS and SaaS will allow us to be able to scale up when needed and to provide access for both individuals and large office like environments. Access can be made through MYSQL where we can manage our data and make any updates if needed. Either Https or FTP can be used to download our program. Two-factor authentication for the Microsoft or Google accounts will be needed to access all profiles. We will include encryption software from DevGlan where it can only be access by the user. The tool could be coded in something like C++ using Microsoft Visual Studio or Java using the Oracle Java Development Kit.


Marketing

We believe that this program has the opportunity to help millions of users who use Windows operating systems. This program can, with an easy to use and pleasing user interface and an instructional step by step guide, make changing computers and reinstalling windows worries a thing of the past.


Many workplaces and schools in today's world have moved to remote working and learning, this means that almost every person needs some sort of computer device whether that be for school or work. This program, like a password manager, would act as not only a way to save your settings but as a security tool should anything happen to their systems or just someone clicked the wrong link in an email. We believe that this tool could be invaluable to schools and businesses as it allows them to have a baseline default setting for all their computers that they can use so if they do need to reset a computer whether that be because the computer has been breached or swapping over a computer to another student or employee.


We believe there is a market for this tool and we can aim the program at first small businesses, schools that don’t have a large IT support staff as well as broadly individual users. We can also market this tool to IT support companies who would also run the support for these small businesses and schools.


Outcome

With the ever-fastening pace with today’s modern world, when we have the option to be able to save a couple of hours, we will. This concept is not only limited to large corporations to cut costs, but down to the individual level, where we have that little bit of extra time to spend without family or to focus on our hobbies. With AusDevs Windows profile tool we hope to be able to provide that option to the user.


With 350 million Windows installed every year, we see a massive opportunity to re-define how we can personalise and secure our interface profile. The tedious task of reinstalling all software and program needed to operate your system the way you like it, will be a thing of the past with our all-in-one transfer program.


Users would also have the ability access others profile setting and will be able to test out a seemingly endless number of options to further personalize their PC.


The massive amount of man hours saved in the corporate world translates directly into saved money. The extra funds may be used to increase productivity and boost efficiency or to finance project that can have an impact on our day to day lives.
